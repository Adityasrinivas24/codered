Frequently Asked Questions

What is
BRO - CODE ‘24 ?

BRO - CODE ‘24 is a 24-hour overnight hackathon featuring real-world company problem statements that aim to bridge academics and industry, encouraging participants to solve practical challenges faced by the organizations

How do I get there?

The event is held at the
BMS Institute of Technology and Managament

When will BRO - CODE ‘24 take place?

BRO - CODE ‘24 is scheduled on 13th - 14th JANUARY 2024

Is it a remote or an on-site Hackathon?

It's a hybrid hackathon, Preliminary round will be conducted completely online. The round 1 i.e, The Main Hackathon will be conducted in
BMSIT&M campus

What is the maximum and minimum number of participants allowed in a group?

A group can have a minimum of 3 participants and a maximum of 4 participants.

Who can apply for BRO - CODE ‘24?

Students from all colleges and universities pursuing any undergraduate course are welcome to apply for the event.

Are travel expenses included?

No, the participants are responsible for covering their own travel expenses.

Does BRO - CODE ‘24 have any restrictions on the programming language to be used?

Nope, you are free to use whatever tech stack/programming language you please.

What kind of skill set is required for BRO - CODE ‘24?

There is no such skill required as a prerequisite for the hackathon. Although we do hope to see the skill of innovation among the participants.

Will free WiFi be provided?

Yes, Wi-Fi access will be shared with all participants.

Is it possible for us to utilize open-source resources to replicate the project code?

The solution may include the use of open-source software, but they should ensure that:
(a) They comply with applicable licenses,
(b) The applicable license terms should not require the release, disclosure, or distribution of any part of the Application back to the open-source community or any third party under any circumstances.

Are there any restrictions on the use of pre-built libraries and packages?

Technically no, you can use as many packages, libraries, and modules as you want. But your contribution to your project will outweigh everything else during the final evaluation.

Do I need to demo my project during the event?

You will be required to demonstrate your project and share your work progress several times during the event. These demonstrations will be considered for grading and Evaluation.
