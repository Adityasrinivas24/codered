Frequently Asked Questions

What is
CODE RED ‘24 ?

CODE RED ‘24 is a 24-hour hackathon where we'll be solving real-world problems through coding. Also remember caffeine is our co-founder, sleep is for the weak, and innovation is for the wickedly brilliant.

Where is it happening?

CODE RED ‘24 is all set to be hosted at
BMS Institute of Technology and Managament, Yelahanka

When will CODE RED ‘24 take place?

It is scheduled on the 13th and 14th of JANUARY 2024

Is it a remote or an on-site Hackathon?

CODE RED ‘24 is a hybrid hackathon, where preliminary round will be conducted completely online. The Main Hackathon will be conducted in
BMSIT&M campus

What is the team size?

A team can have a minimum of 3 and a maximum of 4 participants.

Who can apply for CODE RED ‘24?

We don't care if your GPA is higher than your daily caffeine intake or if you once wrote an essay on the philosophical implications of pizza toppings –
as long as you're a student pursuing any undergraduate course, you're in!

Can I be part of two or more teams?

While we appreciate your ambition, unfortunately, one genius per team. We're not quite ready for parallel universes... yet!

Are travel expenses included?

No, the participants are responsible for covering their own travel expenses.

Can I code in any language?

Feel free to code in any language you fancy. We believe in linguistic liberation—no language restrictions here. Whether you're fluent in Python, proficient in Java, or even speak binary, your code is welcome to join the multilingual party!

Will free WiFi be provided?

Yes, Wi-Fi access will be shared with all participants.

What it takes to conquer
CODE RED ‘24

No need to check off a skill list to enter the hackathon—it's not a grocery store. However, we've got our fingers crossed for a dazzling display of innovation. Think of it as a creativity party—BYO (Bring Your Own) ingenuity, and let's code up a storm!

Can open-source resources be used to replicate the project code?

The solution may include the use of open-source software, but they should ensure that:
(a) They comply with applicable licenses,
(b) The applicable license terms should not require the release, disclosure, or distribution of any part of the Application back to the open-source community or any third party under any circumstances.

Are there any restrictions on the use of pre-built libraries and packages?

While there are no technical restrictions on the usage of packages, libraries, and modules, the primary focus during the final evaluation will be on the significance of your contribution to the overall project

Do I need to demo my project during the event?

You will be required to demonstrate your project and share your work progress several times during the event. These demonstrations will be considered for grading and Evaluation.

Will you provide any computers to work with?

We prefer participants to carry their own devices and tools for the event. If required, arrangements for computer facilities can be made on the campus.

Are you sure I won't get bored or lose motivation?

Worried about losing motivation? Fear not! We've got more energy than a caffeinated squirrel and more fun than a monkey with a keyboard. Boredom says goodbye at
CODE RED ‘24.

Do you have a code of conduct?

Yes! We will be following the Code of Conduct.
TLDR: Be respectful.

Have some other questions?

You can email us to any questions you still have.
